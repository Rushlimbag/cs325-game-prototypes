Black Ice

  • Originality: I decided to attempt simulating ice physics in a game where you have to dodge falling objects.
  • Technical Merit: I added "ice" physics (sliding when stopping), although I could not get sliding to work when changing direction/accelerating.
  • Prototype Postmortem: I learned about collsion detection and basic movement based physics. The hardest part of making this prototype was figuring out how to get collision for each ice spike.

  • High Concept: Dodge falling ice whilst sliding around.
  • Theme: Ice
  • Mandated Variety: Keyboard, a lot of randomness, achiever, platformer
  • Prototype Goal: "Ice" physics and collision detection of multiple objects.
  • Player Experience Goals: A challenging fight against physics and randomness.
  • Gameplay: Left and right arrow keys move the player character, though the character will only slowly lose momentum when the player releases a key. Spacebar can be used to jump. The goal is to dodge as many falling ice spikes as possible (until ~200 fall). If the player is hit by a spike it is gameover.
  • Strategies: Qucikly strafing left and right will negate some of the momentum.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: You play as [PLACEHOLDER], an inquisitive adventurer braving the harsh climes of the arctic circle. While tracking what you believe to be a snow leopard , you find yourself deep inside an icy crevasse. You must try to survive as the ice around you melts, loosening deadly icicles from high above you.
  • Target Audience: People who enjoy difficult platformers like IWBTG.
  • Play Time: A few minutes.
